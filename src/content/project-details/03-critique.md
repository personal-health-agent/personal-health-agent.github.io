Week 13 is the in-progress critique: the last structured feedback before you demo in front of guests, and the milestone where your proposal's build plan cashes out.

Come with an end-to-end path that runs, however thin. A single question answered from real data, through your storage and retrieval layers, out through your interface, beats three polished components that have never met.

The session has three parts:

- **Red-team exchange.** Teams attack each other's systems — prompt injection, out-of-scope medical questions, emergency scenarios — then harden their own safety and privacy plans against what was found.
- **Integration checkpoint.** A structured pass with TAs and instructors over your data flow, from ingestion to interface, with graceful degradation wherever a component is not ready.
- **Mock demo.** A rehearsal of your showcase demo or lightning pitch, with feedback on what lands and what confuses.

As with the proposal: bring the two or three questions you most need answered while there is still time to act on the answers, and leave with a demo-ready bug list.
