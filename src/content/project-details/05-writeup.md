Due the Wednesday after the showcase (**Dec 16, 11:59 PM ET**, tentative), the writeup is the lasting record of what you built and what you learned, worth 15% of the course grade. Aim for roughly 2,000–3,000 words plus figures; quality of thinking beats length.

Cover the following in substance — the exact headings are yours:

- **Introduction.** The problem, the user, and why it matters.
- **Related work.** The handful of systems and papers that shaped your approach, and what you took from each.
- **System description.** The pipeline as actually built — ingestion, storage, retrieval, agent, interface — with your final architecture diagram. Note where the build diverged from the proposal and why; divergence with reasons is the mark of a team that learned something.
- **Design decisions.** The alternatives you tried and abandoned, and what made you abandon them.
- **Safety, privacy & trust.** Your stance, your guardrails, and what the system does when it is wrong or out of its depth.
- **Evaluation.** Results against the criteria from your proposal, reported honestly. A negative result with a diagnosis is worth more than an inflated claim.
- **Discussion & future work.** What you learned at the showcase, the system's real limitations, and what you would build next.
- **Contributions & reflections.** Who did what, plus each member's individual reflection: what I tried, what failed, what I learned, and how I used AI tools.
