order: 1
---

Teams of 3–4 build a working Personal Health Assistant across the full term. The project carries **80% of the course grade**, earned in stages rather than at one final deadline — from the proposal through the showcase to the written technical report. The remaining 20% comes from weekly labs, and several of those labs are where the early project milestones happen.

| Milestone | When | What's due |
|-----------|------|------------|
| Team + one-paragraph idea | Week 4 · Oct 2 | A roster, and a paragraph naming your user, their question, your data, the core interaction, and the demo moment |
| Project-direction note | Week 6 · Oct 16 | A one-page direction note, workshopped with TAs and peers in lab |
| Design studio artifacts | Week 8 · Oct 30 | A storyboard, a core user flow, and a low-fidelity prototype, rolled into a one-page project sketch |
| **Proposal presentation + written proposal** (15%) | Week 9 · Nov 6 | A 5-minute presentation built around a tangible design artifact; the written proposal follows the Wednesday after |
| Integration checkpoint + mock demo | Week 13 · Dec 4 | An end-to-end system run, a red-team exchange, and a mock demo |
| **Final showcase: demo** (20%) | Week 14 · Dec 11 | A lightning pitch, a poster, and a live or recorded demo |
| **Final implementation** (30%) | Week 14 · Dec 11 | The working system behind that demo |
| **Final writeup + reflection** (15%) | Wed, Dec 16 (tentative) | A technical report with individual reflections |

Rows without a weight are graded as that week's lab submission. They are how the project stays on schedule without extra deadlines.

Whatever you build must integrate, at minimum: a wearable, self-report, or behavioral data source (a clinical or synthetic clinical record source is optional); a data storage and retrieval component; an LLM, RAG, or agent component; a user-facing interface or interaction flow; a safety, privacy, and trust consideration; and an evaluation or testing plan.

If you want a starting point, the four example tracks on the [course page](https://personal-health-agent.github.io/#project) — contextual medical scribe, metabolic coach, mental health companion, lab results translator — each name a user, a data source, a core interaction, and a demo moment. Those are the same four things your own idea needs.
